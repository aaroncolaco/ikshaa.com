About

A tour through the home is like a journey through India's rich and varied cultural past. The courtyard is a blend of Goa's Luso-Moorish architectural style. The front, with its high plinth, broad windows, welcoming foyer and ornamental façade, is so typical of the traditional landlord's casa and yet has a distinction all of its own. But then again, you may wonder if you are in a temple in South India: the furniture, the natural rock stone used in the flooring, the red-clay roof tiles and wooden rafters, and the old Tamil wooden pillars in the courtyard all create that aura.

In leafy Loutolim, you’ll feel cocooned from the hard commercialism of more ‘touristy’ North Goa. Instead, Ikshaa, like the rest of Loutolim, is all about that state of mind Goans like to call ‘sussegado’. Here, in Loutolim, you'll find the Goa that once was. It's also where you'll find the Goan - in all his native simplicity. But, for all her solitude and rustic allure, Loutolim is only a 20-minute drive from South Goa’s white-sand beaches. So grab your sombrero, your swim-suit and your sun tan. Don’t bother about feni (the local água da vida) – there’s lots of it in Loutolim, senhor!

Why Ikshaa?

The taste of India continues as you explore Ikshaa: every room is tastefully and artistically appointed with furniture and fabrics from different parts of India. Ikshaa in Sanskrit means 'sight'.
